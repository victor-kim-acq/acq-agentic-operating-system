## Design Context

### Users
Internal operations team of 5-8 people managing a paid membership community (~600 members). They handle member lifecycle, billing, and support daily. The primary job is fast scanning of member data, status triage, and navigating to external tools (HubSpot, Recharge, Stripe). Users are power users — they value density and speed over onboarding or visual hand-holding.

### Brand Personality
Clean, professional, utilitarian. The interface should feel like a well-organized control room — calm, confident, and efficient. No consumer-facing flair or marketing energy.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Refined neutral palette. Slate and gray foundations with functional accent colors reserved for status and data semantics.
- **Status colors**: Green (#10b981) for active/healthy, red for cancelled/error, amber (#f59e0b) for warning/at-risk. These are the only colors that should "pop."
- **Theme**: Light mode only. White card surfaces on a light slate (#f8fafc) background.
- **Typography**: Inter for UI text, DM Mono for data/stats. Favor smaller type sizes (text-xs, text-sm) for high density. Bold/semibold reserved for headings and primary labels.
- **Anti-references**: No gradients, no decorative illustrations, no rounded-pill buttons, no playful colors. This is not a consumer SaaS onboarding flow.
- **References**: Linear, Vercel dashboard, Retool — dense, scannable, professional internal tools.

### Design Principles
1. **Density over decoration** — Pack information tightly. Every pixel should earn its place. Prefer compact spacing and smaller text over generous whitespace.
2. **Scannable hierarchy** — Use weight, size, and muted color to create clear visual hierarchy. The eye should find the answer before the brain forms the question.
3. **Functional color only** — Color communicates state (active, cancelled, warning), not brand or personality. Neutral palette everywhere else.
4. **Link to source of truth** — When displaying data from external systems (HubSpot, Recharge, Stripe), always make it one click to get to the canonical record. Blue text with hover underline for all external links.
5. **No surprises** — Consistent patterns across all views. Cards, badges, tables, and timelines should feel like they belong to the same system. Avoid one-off components or novel interactions.
